Welcome to the Supyire Lexicography Project

The project preserves and documents Supyire — producing bilingual dictionaries, a digitized text corpus, training team members, and delivering a smartphone app to make this knowledge broadly accessible.

About the Project

The Supyire Lexicography Project, officially titled “Completion of Supyire Dictionary and Digitized Text Corpus,” is an initiative of the Department of Language, Linguistics, and Translation at Africa International University (AIU).

It builds on decades of linguistic research by Prof. Robert Carlson and collaborators to create comprehensive Supyire–French and Supyire–English dictionaries, an online text database, and a mobile app.

History of Research on Supyire

Research began in the late 1970s under SIL International with Robert Carlson. Over the years the project compiled lexical databases, interlinear texts, and other resources. The New Testament (2008) was published in Supyire, with ongoing translation and documentation work.

Current Toolbox Database

The Toolbox database contains lexical entries, interlinear examples, and texts used to produce dictionaries and the corpus. We are standardizing entries and preparing export to FLEx.

Example of a Text

Kòɔ́rɛ̀ nɛ̀ yíí nàgá. — “The man went to the market.”

Project Goals

  • Produce Supyire–French and Supyire–English dictionaries in print and digital form.
  • Create a smartphone dictionary app.
  • Develop an online Supyire text database for research and cultural use.
  • Standardize and transfer all lexical data from Toolbox to FLEx.
  • Provide linguistic and IT training for project members.

Project Activities

Dictionary Production

  • Standardize Toolbox entries and coding for FLEx export.
  • Revise and correct dictionary entries and translations.
  • Add orthographic details, examples, and grammar sketches.
  • Prepare and publish print and digital editions.

Text Database Development

  • Regularize transcription and interlinearization of texts.
  • Translate and annotate texts linguistically and culturally.
  • Publish database online and integrate it with the dictionary app.

Project Timeline (2025–2031)

2025–2027

Database & Training Phase

Revise and standardize Toolbox entries, add orthographic information, and transfer data to FLEx. Train RS and MO in linguistics and FLEx usage.

2028–2029

Dictionary & App Development

Revise dictionary entries, produce indexes and grammar sketches, and develop a smartphone dictionary app.

2030–2031

Text Database & Publication

Expand and annotate the text corpus (target 450k–500k words), integrate with the dictionary, and publish the database online.

Expected completion: December 2031

Operational Procedures

  • Project operates under AIU’s Department of Languages, Linguistics, and Translation (LLTD).
  • Quarterly progress reports submitted to the Head of Department.
  • Funding from individual and institutional donations; AIU Finance approves disbursements.
  • All publications acknowledge AIU and LLTD.

Risks & Mitigation

  • Training dependency: Project success depends on RS and MO completing training — ensured by mentoring and supervision.
  • Political risks: If travel to Mali is restricted, meetings will be arranged in neighboring countries.
  • Leadership transition: RC will transfer responsibilities to RS & MO progressively.
